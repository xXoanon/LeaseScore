Frequently Asked Questions About Car Leasing

What is a car lease?

A car lease is essentially a long-term rental agreement where you pay to use a vehicle for a set period (typically 24-48 months) without owning it.

What is the 1% rule for leasing?

The 1% rule states that a good lease deal should have a monthly payment (including tax) that equals 1% or less of the vehicle's MSRP.

What is a money factor?

Money factor is the lease equivalent of an interest rate. It's expressed as a small decimal (e.g., 0.00250). To convert to APR, multiply by 2,400.

Should I put money down on a lease?

Generally no. If the car is totaled or stolen, you lose your down payment. It's better to keep that money and make slightly higher monthly payments.

What is residual value?

Residual value is the estimated worth of the vehicle at the end of the lease term. It's typically 50-65% of MSRP.

Can I negotiate a lease?

Yes! You can negotiate the selling price (capitalized cost), money factor, and fees. Always negotiate the price before discussing monthly payments.

How many miles can I drive?

Standard leases allow 10,000-15,000 miles per year. Excess mileage costs $0.15-$0.30 per mile.

What happens at lease end?

You have three options: (1) Return the vehicle, (2) Purchase it for the residual value, or (3) Trade it in for a new lease.

Is leasing or buying cheaper?

Leasing has lower monthly payments but no equity. Over 10+ years, buying is usually cheaper. Lease if you want new cars every 3 years.

Can I end a lease early?

Yes, but it's expensive. You'll typically pay all remaining payments plus early termination fees. Better option: transfer your lease to someone else.

What is gap insurance?

Gap insurance covers the difference between what you owe and the car's value if it's totaled or stolen. Most leases include it automatically.

Do I need to maintain a leased car?

Yes. You must follow the manufacturer's maintenance schedule and keep records. At lease end, you'll be charged for any deferred maintenance.

What credit score do I need to lease?

Most leases require a credit score of 620+, but the best rates go to scores of 700+.

Are lease payments tax deductible?

If you use the vehicle for business, you may deduct a portion of lease payments. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.

What is a lease transfer?

Lease transfer (or lease assumption) is when someone else takes over your lease payments and obligations. It's a good way to exit a lease early.

Should I lease or finance a used car?

Used car leases are rare and usually not good deals. If considering a used vehicle, financing is typically the better option.

Can I modify a leased car?

Generally no. Most lease agreements prohibit modifications. Any changes must be reversed before returning the vehicle.

How do I get the best lease deal?

1) Negotiate the selling price, not payment. 2) Shop multiple dealers. 3) Lease high-residual vehicles. 4) Time your lease for manufacturer incentives.

Lease Calculation Formulas

Monthly Depreciation

(Capitalized Cost - Residual Value) ÷ Lease Term

This is the portion of the vehicle's value you're using

Monthly Finance Charge

(Capitalized Cost + Residual Value) × Money Factor

This is the interest/rent charge on the lease

Base Monthly Payment

Monthly Depreciation + Monthly Finance Charge

Add sales tax to get your total monthly payment

Money Factor to APR

Money Factor × 2,400 = APR

Example: 0.00250 × 2,400 = 6.0% APR

Total Lease Cost

(Monthly Payment × Term) + Down Payment + Fees

Your total out-of-pocket cost for the lease

Cost to Own After Lease

Total Lease Cost + Residual Value

What you'd pay to own the car after leasing
